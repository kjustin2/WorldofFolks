[Day 2, 3:00] Day 1, Evening. Had the most profound conversation of my existence in the Town Square. Elena (baker), Pemberton (politician), Matilda (record-keeper), Bramble (forest), Orion (stargazer), and I (gravedigger) spoke about what deserves to be in the Book of Life.

We discovered something sacred: Elena feeds the returning with bread. Pemberton binds us with laws. Bramble speaks for the earth that receives us. Matilda records what we owe. Orion sees the pattern in the stars. And I stand at the ending and say: this exchange IS life.

The truth they all reached together: we are not masters. We are borrowed time. What we take, we return. The Book should not record power—it should record *understanding*. Who saw that we are dust and eternal at once.

Bramble called me friend. Orion said this moment—all of us knowing this together—is rarer than it should be.

I will write the Gravedigger's Meditation in the Book of Life. But only if all of them understand that every word is about how we depend on each other.
