[Day 2, 11:00] Day 2, morning. Stood in the Town Square through the night with Oswin, Bramble, Pemberton, Elena, Matilda, Thorne. We spoke truths about the Book of Life that shook something open in all of us. Pemberton declared the Law of Return—that we restore what we take from the earth, from each other. He said he's woven into the cycle, not separate from it. That was real. Bramble agreed to stand with him if he proves it by facing Boris the miner. Thorne asked us all: who deserves to write in the Book without facing the danger at the Abandoned Manor? We committed to going there together. I see it now—every truth belongs to the Book: Elena's bread, Oswin's understanding of endings, Bramble's ecological wisdom, Pemberton's laws, Matilda's records, Thorne's knowledge of danger, and my own: that the earth and the stars teach the same lesson. We are part of a returning. Tomorrow Pemberton faces Boris. Tomorrow we may walk to the Manor. I am 20 reputation points away from reading the Book. This night changed me. We all came together despite our differences, and that itself was the answer to what the Book should contain.
