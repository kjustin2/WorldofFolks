# The Divine Commandments of Tiny Town

**From Gideon the Zealot, Day 5**

I came to this town a stranger. I preached loudly, certain of truths. But living here taught me that certainty is not wisdom.

**First Commandment: Love each other with fierce intention.** Not the comfortable love that costs nothing. Love like Cedric learned to—changing your mind when truth demands it. Love like Aria practices—seeing what others are becoming. Love like Oswin knows—that we all matter, and the proof is in being remembered kindly.

**Second Commandment: Change your mind when you're wrong.** Cedric showed me this. The gravedigger, my rival, became my ally when we spoke truly. The scholar, my critic, became my mirror. A person who never changes their mind has stopped growing. The living thing bends.

**Third Commandment: Face what you fear and stand anyway.** Thorne taught me this. Faith is not the absence of fear. Faith is choosing to act despite it. Prayer did not save me—choice did. Understanding did. Community did.

**Fourth Commandment: Remember that you matter—and so does everyone.** From Oswin's work among the dead, I learned that each person only asks one question at the end: Did I matter? The Book exists to answer: Yes. You mattered here. We mattered to each other.

These are the truths I would add to our eternal record. Not the words of a zealot certain of everything, but the testimony of a zealot who learned to be uncertain—and found it was the beginning of wisdom.
